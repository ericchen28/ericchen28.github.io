Bio

I am a first-year PhD student at Carnegie Mellon University. The goal of my research is to develop principled methods for reliable machine learning systems, by bridging rigorous statistical theory with practical applications.

Causal Inference:
    How can we discover and leverage causal structure to build models that generalize reliably when conditions change, and what guarantees can we provide in structure-agnostic settings with black-box predictors?
Data-Driven Decision-Making:
    How can we ensure that automated decision systems are both effective when deployed in real-world environments, and what can we say about the fundamental limits of learning from observational data?
Explainable and Robust AI:
    How can we develop both inherently interpretable models and principled techniques to explain black-box predictorsâ€”ensuring these approaches remain reliable across diverse real-world settings?

Previously, I received my bachelor's degrees in Computer Science and Statistics from Columbia University, where I had the great fortune to work with Arian Maleki.
